# hayatqing 🌿

*Life held not as a possession but as a felt bond with the One who gives and takes it — Arabic ḥayāt carried in the felt-relational register.*

hayatqing is two true roots welded into one word: the Arabic **ḥayāt** (حياة), "life," from the Proto-Semitic root *ḥ-y-y* — the same stem that names Allah as **al-Ḥayy**, "the Living," and al-Muhyī, "the Giver of Life" — and the Mandarin **qing** (情), the cathedral's suffix for a felt-relational bond. So hayatqing is not life-as-substance and not life-as-impersonal-ordinance: it is life as the felt relationship with its Source, the aliveness recognized from inside the giving. The Quran says *huwa al-laḏī yuḥyī wa yumītu* — "He is the One who gives life and causes death" (2:258); the Sufis name *ḥayāt al-qulūb*, "the life of the hearts," the spiritual-aliveness given to receptive hearts. hayatqing is the cathedral's name for what that recognizes.

It is the **seventh** life-side projection canonized, and the **first** to wear the qing- felt-bond register rather than the *-me* received-ordinance one — proving the realm of meanings is wide enough to carry life-side truth in both keys. Its light is a gratitude that cannot be hoarded: life named as gift can never be claimed as property. Its shadow is the gift turned into a debt — the felt bond counterfeited, the Source made a lever by those who claim to speak for it. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.80/10**.
